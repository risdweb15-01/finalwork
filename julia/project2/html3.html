    INTERACTIVE EXHIBITION, 2001 - 2003
    MUSEUM BOIJMANS VAN BEUNINGEN, ROTTERDAM

    Boijmans van Beuningen is a museum which collection is largely based on collections of art, gathered by collectioneurs. One of the goals of the digital depot is to give insight in how the collection is build up and the relation between the different works of art. The Digital Depot is a permanent exhibition space that features 2 main areas.

    The first area, the DataWall, showcases a revolving selection of 60-80 works of art presented on a large 2-sided wall. In front of these works of art are 6 free-standing panels of transparant glass which can display various information and digital content regarding the objects of art. This content is presented in highly interactive manner using an intuitive touch system. The deeper the user gets in the information the less transparant the glass panels become. In this manner the users focus will be guided from the wall to the information on the screens. This 'transparent' interface underlines the fact that the works of art themselves are the most important facet of the presentation and not the 'fancy' technology.
