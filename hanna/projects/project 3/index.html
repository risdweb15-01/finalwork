FACULTY CO-HOSTS
Ijlal Muzaffar

Ijlal Muzaffar is an Assistant Professor of Modern Architectural History at the Rhode Island School of Design. He has also taught at the Program in History, Theory, and Criticism of Architecture and Art at MIT, from where he also received his PhD in 2007. He also holds a Master of Architecture degree form Princeton, and a BA in mathematics and physics from the University of Punjab in Lahore, Pakistan. He is working on a book based on his dissertation that looks at how modern architects and planners played a critical role in shaping the discourse on Third World development and its associated structures of power and intervention in the postwar era.

Matt Kressy

Matthew S. Kressy is principal of Designturn Inc., a product design firm in Wellesley, Massachusetts, Senior Lecturer at MIT Engineering Systems Division, Adjunct Faculty of Industrial Design at the Rhode Island School of Design and Adjunct Faculty at Babson College. He holds a BFA in Industrial Design from the Rhode Island School of Design. He specializes in creative product solutions, including volume production in Asia, through early integration of all related product development disciplines including manufacturing. He has been named director of the Integrated Design and Management (IDM) master's degree track offered by the MIT System Design and Management (SDM) program.
